### 🔥 #1｜Towards Embodied AI with MuscleMimic: Unlocking full-body musculoskeletal motor learning at scale

**Chengkun Li, Cheryl Wang, Bianca Ziliotto 等 (7 位作者)** |  `NVIDIA, EPFL, McGill`

📎 [2603.25544](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25544) | `cs.RO`

> Here we present MuscleMimic, an open-source framework for scalable motion imitation learning with physiologically realistic, muscle-actuated humanoids. Leveraging massively parallel GPU simulation, the framework achieves order-of-magnitude training speedups over prior CPU-based approaches while maintaining comprehensive collision handling, enabling a single generalist policy to be trained on hundreds of diverse motions within days. The resulting policy faithfully reproduces a broad repertoire of human movements under full muscular control and can be fine-tuned to novel motions within hours. Code, models, checkpoints, and retargeted datasets are available at: https://github.com/amathislab/musclemimic
>
> **关键技术：** 模仿学习 / 微调

### 🔥 #5｜Can Users Specify Driving Speed? Bench2Drive-Speed: Benchmark and Baselines for Desired-Speed Conditioned Autonomous Driving

📎 [2603.25672](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25672) | `cs.RO cs.CV`

> End-to-end autonomous driving (E2E-AD) has achieved remarkable progress. However, one practical and useful function has been long overlooked: users may wish to customize the desired speed of the policy or specify whether to allow the autonomous vehicle to overtake. 📎 [2603.25716](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25716) | `cs.CV cs.AI`

> Video world models have shown immense potential in simulating the physical world, yet existing memory mechanisms primarily treat environments as static canvases. When dynamic subjects hide out of sight and later re-emerge, current methods often struggle, leading to frozen, distorted, or vanishing subjects. To address this, we introduce Hybrid Memory, a novel paradigm requiring models to simultaneously act as precise archivists for static backgrounds and vigilant trackers for dynamic subjects, ensuring motion continuity during out-of-view intervals. 📎 [2603.25629](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25629) | `cs.CV cs.LG`

> While language reasoning models excel in many tasks, visual reasoning remains challenging for current large multimodal models (LMMs).
