### 🔥 #3｜Behavior Uncloning: Distilling Mode Redirection into Policy Weights without Inference-Time Steering

**Hao Wang, Jiuzhou Lei, Dayou Li 等 (8 位作者)** |  `Stanford, UW-Madison, Texas A&M, Northwestern`

📎 [2606.29201](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29201) | `cs.RO cs.AI`

> To address this gap, we propose MoRE(Mode Redirection), which redirects policy rollouts toward desired behavior modes through a short "uncloning" step. Specifically, MoRE distills the redirection signal from a temporary mode classifier into the policy weights to steer behavior. A retain loss balances this edit by preserving desired-mode competence, allowing the standalone policy to suppress unwanted modes with zero inference-time overhead. Across eight simulated and real-world tasks, MoRE improves the average deployment success rate (SR) by 44 percentage points over the original mixed-mode policy. Among all compared adaptation and steering baselines, MoRE achieves the strongest SR and approaches the filtered-data retraining reference, while preserving task competence and inference speed
>
